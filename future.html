All the bulbs that lit up for me

As I move onto almost wrapping up my app, and I open all of the created files one more time to check if everything is clean and functional, I look back to realize how many hidden moments are berried under this code in which I was stumped and found a solution in the most bizarre fashion. I started off my making my log in page that separated a student and a teacher taking them to their desired home page, I remembered how many light bulbs switched on inside my head while writing that specific pages JavaScript!

I had a lot of the bright moments Mr. Brown frequently talks about, and primarily these moments occurred during my process of completing the CSS and JavaScript of that specific page. CSS was always a sweet challenge I liked to learn and face throughout this the process of completing my app, and the light bulbs above my head would flash as soon as I saw something align correctly on my page. However, during this process I have a huge regret, which I would have done completely different if I were to start this project again. Specifically this regret being my apps margins not cooperating once the screen was minimized, and now after going through this whole year I have understood the reasoning of this problem. The only way of fixing such margin error was to from the beginning have all the margins of my CSS attributes added on a page set to auto, or a certain percentage once minimized. This is something I look at now at definitely laugh at as I know my mistake, and to show my understanding, in the website I have created for this portfolio I have set margins for all attributes in a manor which makes minimizing the screen a possibility with everything remaining maintained in a decent position

A few steps away

Stepping away from the amazing experiences I had during my construction of my website, My computer science teacher has assigned us with a project that allows us to slightly step away from the coding aspect of the course. Now we have been given another very cool task to complete over the winter vacations, and this time we are working in groups of 4! My group has chosen to investigate the health effects attached to the usage of computers. I completed some heavy research related to LED and CRT monitors. I learned how a Cathode Ray Tube emits radiation, and the process they use to do so. If you own a desktop monitor or even a TV that can be dated of creation a few years back, and you have it stored up in a deep cabinet, chances are it is a CRT. In these types of screens, as the electrons sweep across the surface of the screen, they emit radiation (x-ray frequencies). I like to talk about such projects completed over Computer Science, as this is an aspect that has made my experience in Computer science a whole lot better.
